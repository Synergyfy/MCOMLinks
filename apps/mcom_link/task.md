# 1️⃣ The Big Structure First

We made it clear that:

We are not just labeling campaigns.

We are defining **how the network operates**.

Inside **MCOMQLinks**, there are three operational exposure layers:

1. **National**
2. **Hyper-local**
3. **Nearby**

Each one has:

* A different purpose
* A different level of control
* A different business use case
* A different rotator logic

And importantly:

> 247GBS must control what runs on the network.

---

# 2️⃣ NATIONAL – How We Said It Will Be Used

## What It Is

National is:

* Broad
* Sector-based
* Not postcode specific
* Controlled by 247GBS

It is used for:

* 247GBS offers
* Corporate campaigns
* Digital products
* National promotions
* Network-wide messaging

We said National is powerful because:

* It allows us to push campaigns across the entire network.
* It is suitable for corporate brands that want nationwide exposure.
* It is not tied to a single high street.

## Where It Runs

National campaigns run on:

* Storefront assets
* QR placements
* Rotator placements
* Any 247GBS-controlled asset

Unless overridden.

## Control Logic

National campaigns:

* Are default content on many storefronts.
* Run until paused.
* Can be switched off when a paid campaign is inserted.
* Must not break live purchases or active sessions.

We stressed:

We must design a system that allows:

* Switching National off
* Running paid campaigns
* Then restoring National

Without causing operational issues.

---

# 3️⃣ HYPER-LOCAL – How We Said It Will Be Used

## What It Is

Hyper-local is:

* Postcode-based
* Location-based
* Tied to a specific storefront
* Tied to a specific business owner

It is very specific.

Example:
Toby Barbers in Peckham.

Hyper-local = Peckham storefront exposure.

## Who Uses It

Used when:

* A business owner is promoting their own product or service.
* A sponsor is targeting a specific postcode.
* A storefront location is active and claimed.

Hyper-local campaigns are about:

* Promoting offers to consumers within a specific high street.
* Driving traffic to a specific shop.
* Activating local engagement.

## Relationship to Storefront

When a business owner claims their storefront:

* That storefront becomes an asset.
* That asset can run hyper-local campaigns.
* That asset can be controlled by rotator logic.

We made it clear:

Hyper-local requires admin approval.

Business owners cannot freely publish without oversight.

---

# 4️⃣ NEARBY – How We Said It Will Be Used

Nearby is the most strategic layer.

## What It Is

Nearby is:

* Not national.
* Not strictly postcode locked.
* Used primarily for B2B engagement.
* A business owner expansion tool.

We said:

Nearby allows a business owner to reach beyond their own high street.

Example:

Toby Barbers (Peckham)
Runs a Nearby campaign targeting:

* Brixton
* Stratford
* Balham

This is B2B outreach.

## Purpose of Nearby

Nearby allows business owners to:

* Form partnerships
* Promote services to other businesses
* Offer cross-promotions
* Expand influence without going fully national

We clarified:

Technically it is similar to hyper-local.

But the difference is:

* The intent (B2B expansion)
* The label
* The targeting radius

Nearby is a sweet spot:

* It gives business owners flexibility.
* It does not interfere with national control.
* It does not remove admin oversight.

## Control Logic

Even Nearby campaigns:

* Must be approved.
* Must be controlled.
* Cannot run freely.

Business owners must:

* Pay for ads
  OR
* Have a membership tier that includes ad access.

* Support switching between National and Paid campaigns.
* Support group campaigns.
* Support weighted campaigns (e.g., 60% Toby, 40% partners).
* Support split logic when needed.
* Support sequential logic when needed.
* Support seasonal logic.

The rotator must understand:

* Which mode is active.
* Which campaign is priority.
* Which layer has override rights.
